# Areas of Focus & Responsibility

> Life areas that require ongoing attention but are never "done." These bridge vision and projects — when an area has a gap, it generates a new project.

## Physical Health
*Keeping the body strong, energized, and capable*
- Regular exercise (strength training, cardio, flexibility)
- Nutrition that supports energy and recovery
- Adequate sleep and recovery time
- Regular health check-ups and monitoring

## Spiritual Life
*Nurturing faith and inner life*
- Daily practice (prayer, meditation, reflection)
- Study and reading
- Community participation
- Living values in daily decisions

## Family
*Being present and caring for family*
- Regular contact and communication
- Celebrating milestones and holidays
- Supporting through challenges
- Honoring those who came before

## Community & Relationships
*Being a good neighbor and friend*
- Local community participation
- Helping others when needs arise
- Maintaining friendships through regular contact
- Being reliable and present

## Finances
*Living wisely within means*
- Budget monitoring and awareness
- Frugal choices that align with values
- Savings goals on track
- Spending aligned with priorities

## Creativity & Learning
*Feeding the mind and creative spirit*
- Regular creative practice (music, writing, art)
- Skill development and deliberate practice
- Reading and exploration
- Trying new tools and approaches

## Outdoor Adventure
*Getting outside regularly*
- Regular outdoor activities (hiking, cycling, swimming)
- Seasonal pursuits
- Nature as medicine and reset
- Physical challenges that test limits

## Home & Environment
*Keeping living space simple, functional, and peaceful*
- Minimalism — reduce clutter, keep what matters
- Regular maintenance and organization
- Digital environment hygiene
- Space supports focus and calm

## Productivity & Systems
*Maintaining the trusted system*
- Weekly review discipline
- Daily rituals (morning and evening)
- Inbox processing habits
- Tools serve the workflow

## Rest & Recovery
*Recharging so other areas get your best*
- Scheduled downtime
- Energy management (not just time management)
- Sleep quality and consistency
- Activities that restore energy
